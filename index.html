Izaskun Mallona

Computational biologist specialized in cancer epigenomics. Designer of innovative approaches to integrate and visualize massive and heterogeneous biomedical data, including the information storage and retrieval speed up (engineering), the hypothesis evaluation facilitation (visualization and analysis) and the dissection of biological-question-driven patterns (mining).

Released multiple user-friendly tools addressed to biomedical researchers and clinicians to process a wide variety of data, including The Cancer Genome Atlas (TCGA), ENCODE, bisulphite sequencing, de novo Expressed Sequence Tags (ESTs) and Real Time PCR experiments.

Current main projects include a framework to analyze the DNA co-methylation networks for pancancer data; and the evaluation of the functional role of repetitive elements from an evolutionary point of view.
