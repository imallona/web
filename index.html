I am a computational biologist working with transcriptomics, epigenomics, and multi‑omics datasets. My work focuses on developing reproducible workflows and methods, benchmarking computational methods, and supporting collaborative projects with experimental and clinical groups. I have worked with bulk and single‑cell data in transcriptomics, proteomics, variant calling, and epigenomics. A list of my publications and datasets is available through my ORCID profile. My main research driver is fulfilling scientific curiosity.

I develop modular analysis pipelines in R, bash, Python, make, and Snakemake; maintain workflows on high‑performance computing systems, and work on reproducible evaluation frameworks for bioinformatics tools. I semi-officially lead Omnibenchmark, a tool providing automations and abstractions for benchmarking computational methods, mostly in bioinformatics. Additional code, workflows, and documentation are available on GitHub and Bitbucket.

My broader interests include free software, open science, transparent reporting, and the design of computational infrastructure to support long-term service and reproducibility. I have collaborated with multiple European research groups on projects in biomedical research, developmental genetics, neuroscience, and pure method development, and mentored dozens of students, particularly women.
